Tourism & Dive Industry Web Design

Dive Shop Website Design in the Philippines

Home / Dive Shop Websites

If you run a dive shop, dive resort, or tourism operator in the Philippines, your website has one job: get a stranger from "I found this on Google" to "I just booked a dive trip." Most dive shop sites fail at this because they were built as generic templates with stock photos and no thought for how divers actually search and book.

What a dive shop website actually needs

Having built sites for operators on Panglao (Bohol Diving Adventures) and Malapascua Island (Malapascua Diving Adventures), the pattern that converts is consistent:

  • Dive site pages with real depth, conditions, and what divers will see — this is what international divers actually search for ("Kimud Shoal thresher shark dive," "Balicasag Island diving").
  • Transparent pricing in Philippine Peso, broken down by package — divers comparing operators will skip pages that hide pricing behind a "contact us" form.
  • WhatsApp or Messenger booking — most Philippine dive bookings still happen over chat, not a checkout cart. A booking form that routes straight to WhatsApp converts better than a generic contact form for this audience.
  • PADI/SSI certification badges and reviews — trust signals matter enormously for an activity involving safety.
  • Mobile-first design — most dive trip research happens on a phone, often on slow island wifi, so page speed isn't optional.

Common mistakes I see in dive shop sites

The biggest one is treating the dive site list as an afterthought — a single paragraph instead of individual pages. Each dive site (Gato Island, Kimud Shoal, Balicasag, Pamilacan, Cabilao) is a distinct search term with real, specific search volume from divers planning trips. A site with one page per major dive site, written with real local detail, will out-rank a competitor's generic "we dive everywhere" page almost every time.

The second mistake is slow, oversized photo galleries. Underwater photography is gorgeous but huge raw files will tank your Core Web Vitals and your mobile rankings. Every image needs to be compressed and lazy-loaded.

What's included when I build a dive shop site

A typical package covers a homepage, services/pricing section, dedicated dive site pages, an about/trust section, FAQ with schema markup (so questions can appear directly in Google search results), and a WhatsApp-first contact flow — plus on-page SEO and Search Console setup so the site is actually discoverable from day one, not just live.
