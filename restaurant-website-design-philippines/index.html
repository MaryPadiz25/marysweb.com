Restaurant Website Design in the Philippines

Home / Restaurant Websites

Most small restaurants in the Philippines rely entirely on a Facebook page or Google Business Profile — and that's a real gap. A proper website gives you something Facebook can't: control over how you're found on Google, and a fast, professional menu page that converts a hungry searcher into a customer walking through your door (or placing an order).

What actually matters for a restaurant website

  • A real HTML menu, not a PDF or photo — Google can't read text inside a PDF or image well, and on mobile, PDFs are a frustrating download. An HTML menu page can rank for "[restaurant name] menu" and "[dish] [your town]" searches.
  • Location, hours, and map embedded directly — don't make people leave your site to find your address; this also helps local SEO and Google Maps visibility.
  • Mobile speed — most people search for a place to eat from their phone, often while already out. A slow-loading site loses that customer to the next Google result.
  • Photos that load fast — food photography sells, but uncompressed images are one of the most common reasons restaurant sites load slowly.
  • Clear calls to action — a phone number, a Messenger/WhatsApp link, or a reservation form, placed where people will actually see it, not buried at the bottom.

Local SEO for restaurants

A website alone isn't enough — pairing it with a fully filled-out Google Business Profile (hours, photos, menu, categories, and genuine customer reviews) is what actually drives "restaurant near me" traffic in the Philippines. I set this up alongside every restaurant site I build, along with basic schema markup that tells Google explicitly that you're a restaurant, what cuisine you serve, and your price range — improving how you appear in search results.

What's included

A typical restaurant package includes a homepage, full menu page, photo gallery, location/hours section with map, and a contact or reservation form — plus on-page SEO, schema markup, and Google Business Profile setup so the site actually gets found, not just built.
